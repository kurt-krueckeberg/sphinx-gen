# 25 August 1849 Public Notice of Emigration of Johann Heinrich Krückeberg to America

In many German states, the names of people intending to emigrate were
officially published before they departed so that creditors could bring
claims before the local authorities. In Schaumburg-Lippe, these notices
appeared in the Anzeigen des Fürstenthums Schaumburg-Lippe (Notices of the
Principality of Schaumburg-Lippe), the principality’s official publication
for government notices, orders, and announcements. They were especially
important when an emigrant had sold property, because the sale proceeds
could be used to satisfy outstanding claims.


By 8 August 1849, Johann Heinrich Krückeberg had already reached an
agreement with the buyer, the Einlieger Wilhelm Bornemann of Nordholz,
regarding the purchase price for Stätte No. 10 in Berenbusch, and he had
reported that agreement to the Amt.[^krueckeberg-sale-file] The Amt
Bückeburg then published notice of Krückeberg’s intended emigration and
sale in the 25 August 1849 issue of the *Anzeigen des Fürstenthums
Schaumburg-Lippe*, summoning anyone with claims against him to appear
before the Amt on 5 September 1849. The Kaufbrief was subsequently
submitted on 8 September and officially approved by the Rentkammer on 20
September 1849.

## Transliteration

The section header **Bekanntmachung** (Notice) appears in the left column
of page 204 of the **Schaumburg-Lippe: Anzeigen des Fürstenthums Schaumburg-Lippe** for the year **1849**


```text
Bückeburg. Der Colon Krückelberg No 10
in Berenbusch, welcher nach Amerika
auszuwandern im Begriff steht, hat
seine Stätte an den Einlieger Wilhelm
Bornemann auf N 14 in Nordholz
verkauft, und werden auf Antrag des
erstern alle diejenigen, welche Forderun-
gen an ihn haben hierdurch aufgefor-
dert, zur Anmeldung derselben
am 5. k. M. September
Morgens 11 Uhr vor hiesigem Amte
zu erscheinen und Befriedigung zu ge-
wärtigen.
Bückeburg, den 23. August 1849.
Fürstlich Schaumb.-Lippisches Amt.

```
