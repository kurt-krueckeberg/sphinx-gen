Hans Heinrich Wilharm was born on [27 August 1760](PET-B-1760a.md). On

Together they had three daughters together, the first daughter was Anne
Marie Philippine Blecke, who was born on 11 July 1773. She married Hans

She [died on 11 April 1797](PET-D-1797a.md), the day after their

After Hans Heinrich Wilharms first wife, Anne Marie Philippine Blecke died,
he married her sister, Sophie Ernestine Blecke, on [27

Bleeke was the father of both of the Blecke sisters who married Hans
Heinrich Wilharm. He died in 1779, and she remarried, on 11 June 1780, to

:::{admonition} Meaning of Einlieger

**“Schneider und Einlieger in Nr. 14 in Bärenbusch”** tells us two separate
things: his trade (tailor) and his social/tenurial position (Einlieger). An
Einlieger in early-19th-century rural Germany was typically a lodger or
subtenant without a full farm or house place of their own—part of the
unterbäuerliche (sub-peasant) strata who lived “in” an existing holding rather
than as the recognized holder of it.

So “Einlieger in Nr. 14” very strongly suggests that Carl Friedrich Gottlieb
Krückeberg did not own or inherit farm no. 14, but instead rented rooms or a
small secondary dwelling on that property, supporting himself as a tailor while
living in the household (or on the land) of whoever was the actual
Colon/householder of No. 14 in Berenbusch.

:::
